The Audit Gap in AI Systems

Regulators don't ask "is your AI smart?" — they ask "can you prove what it did, why, and with what evidence?"

⚠️

No Evidence Chain

Most AI systems produce answers without traceable evidence. Auditors cannot verify what sources informed a decision.

⚠️

No Tamper Detection

Without SHA-256 artifact hashing, there is no way to prove that an AI report has not been modified after generation.

⚠️

No Access Control

Without RBAC and IDOR protection, AI thread data can be accessed by unauthorized users — a critical audit failure.

Auxtho closes every one of these gaps.

Regulatory Compliance

For Banks & MAS Tier 1 Auditors

Answers to the questions every regulator will ask about your AI governance.

"How do you handle non-indexed sources?"

All evidence is sourced exclusively from the indexed Knowledge Vault. Non-indexed content is explicitly excluded from AI reasoning.

Indexing Policy: Documents are ingested via controlled upload with CO approval. Refresh cycle runs daily at 00:00 UTC. Every indexed source carries a content hash and ingestion timestamp.

Scope Limitation: Auxtho explicitly declares scope boundaries in every exported report. Non-indexed sources are flagged as out-of-scope.

✅ Implemented

"Can trust scores be manipulated at runtime?"

No. Trust score weights and thresholds are policy-bound and cannot be changed at runtime.

Policy Binding: Weights are fixed per policy version. Any modification requires a new policy version with explicit approval and audit trail.

Verification: Each exported report includes the policy version hash used during generation, enabling independent verification that the correct weights were applied.

✅ Implemented

"How do you govern model and pipeline changes?"

All model and embedding pipeline versions are tracked and locked per policy version. No silent upgrades.

Version Governance: Model changes (LLM provider, embedding model) require a policy version update with approval chain. Current versions are stamped on every exported artifact.

Reproducibility: Given the same policy version and input, the system enforces deterministic governance controls — same evidence retrieval path, same trust score calculation, same output structure.

✅ Implemented

"What is your data retention and chain-of-custody policy?"

Audit logs, evidence chains, and exported artifacts are designed to support up to 7-year retention requirements with tamper-evident SHA-256 hashing. PII minimized and redacted per policy.

Chain of Custody: Every action in the system — from login to evidence retrieval to export — is logged with user ID, timestamp, and action type. PII minimized and redacted per policy (including hashing where applicable).

Integrity: Exported PDF artifacts carry a SHA-256 artifact hash that can be independently verified. PKCS#7 CMS detached signing + RFC 3161 timestamp is a planned milestone.

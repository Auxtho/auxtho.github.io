Why AI outputs fail review

Most failures are not model-quality failures. They are control failures that appear after a draft is generated and before a result is treated as releasable.

No release control

High-impact outputs move forward without explicit review or sign-off before release.

No evidence chain

Claims are produced without clear source linkage, approval context, or policy traceability.

No integrity signal

Artifacts can be changed after export with no visible reference for later checking.

Governance controls

Four controls that move a useful draft toward a review-ready release artifact.

Approval

Human review gate

Release remains blocked until a reviewer makes the approval decision.

Evidence

Evidence-linked output

Outputs remain tied to the references, timestamps, and review context used to produce them.

Integrity

Artifact integrity reference

Artifacts carry a reference that can later be checked during verification or reconstruction.

Access

Role-bound access

Access and release behavior remain constrained by role and workflow state.

Why this matters

Under scrutiny, the question is not whether the draft was useful. It is whether the released artifact can still be explained, checked, and reviewed later.

Verification in practice

  1. Open the exported artifact and confirm the approval state.
  2. Check the linked evidence and integrity reference against the recorded context.
  3. Reconstruct the release path if a reviewer challenges the artifact later.

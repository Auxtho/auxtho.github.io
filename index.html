Why AI Programs Fail Audit

Most failures are not model quality issues. They are control failures.

No release control

High-impact outputs move forward without explicit compliance sign-off.

No evidence chain

Outputs are generated without source-level traceability or policy binding.

No integrity proof

Artifacts can be changed after export with no independent tamper signal.

MAS TRM-ALIGNED EXPECTATIONS

Audit Readiness Responses

Condensed answers to the controls audit teams usually test first.

"How do you prevent non-approved sources from influencing outputs?"

Evidence is restricted to indexed sources under policy-bound retrieval rules. Out-of-scope content is not treated as supporting evidence.

Control: Indexed evidence scope, versioned policy binding, and audit logging on retrieval events.

Active

"Can scoring logic be altered silently at runtime?"

Scoring and threshold behavior is tied to policy versions. Changes require a policy update event and audit record.

Control: Policy version identifiers are stamped on exported artifacts for reproducibility checks.

Active

"How is chain-of-custody maintained over retention periods?"

System actions are logged with actor and timestamp metadata, and artifact hashes support post-export integrity checks.

Control: SHA-256 artifact integrity is active. PKCS#7 CMS and RFC 3161 timestamp verification remain planned milestones.
